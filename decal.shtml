Course Description: An introductory course to Quantum Computing. We will introduce ideas of qubits and quantum algorithms while also exploring topics in quantum information theory. Furthermore, we will teach students the state of the art and latest developments in the field.

Learning Goals: Students taking this course will learn the fundamentals of quantum computing and be equipped for further study in the field and for research and industry.

Prerequisites: Linear Algebra (Math 54, Physics 89, EECS 16A/B)
Preferred: Experience with Python (CS61A, DATA8, CS88)
Beneficial for a deeper understanding: Quantum Mechanics (Physics 137A), Algorithms (CS170), Advanced Linear Algebra (Math 110)

Textbooks: Course slides/lecture notes, Vazirani lecture notes. Optional: Nielsen, Chuang; Rieffel, Polak.

Midterm: One midterm evaluation will test that students have a strong understanding of the fundamentals before proceeding to more advanced quantum algorithms.

Homework: A mix of coding and math exercises will reinforce and test ideas from lecture as students learn new concepts.

Math Topics: Vectors, matrix multiplication, bases, dot product, complex numbers, tensor product, diagonalization, eigenvectors

Special Topics: Adiabatic Quantum Computing, Quantum Annealing, Spin, qubit implementations, topological/ion trap/superconducting qubits, Tensors and tensor networks
