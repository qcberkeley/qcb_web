Completed Projects

Particle Reconstruction Using QML

A Quantum Machine Learning model taking in information about a particle collision or decay event (energy, momentum, etc.) and outputting attributes about the particle(s) in the interaction (mass, charge, spin, etc.).

Prescribing Color to Quantum States

Building on a method developed at UC Berkeley that uses color to represent quantum states, addressing limitations of the Bloch Sphere for multi-qubit, mixed, and entangled states.

Sentiment Analysis Using QNN

A RNN based on quantum circuits promises to avoid the vanishing gradients problem and may perform better on standard NLP tasks. We applied quantum RNNs to a sentiment-analysis task, categorizing product reviews as positive or negative to validate the approach and demonstrate theoretical advantages.

View Presentation

Variational Quantum Eigensolvers — A Review of NISQ Era Algorithms

We implemented and evaluated VQE algorithms in the context of NISQ-era quantum hardware constraints.

View Presentation

COVID-19 and QAOA

We used a quantum annealer to determine how to safely reopen college campuses via quarantining students, and implemented QAOA on Qiskit and TensorFlow Quantum, showing numerically that for level-1 QAOA similar parameters are optimal on Erdős–Rényi and regular graphs.

Quarantine Results GitHub Repo

Partial Quantum Search — Spring '19

We implemented a variation of Grover's canonical search algorithm known as partial quantum search in PyQuil (a Python quantum computing library by Rigetti).

Qubit Simulation Project

The Qubit Project constructed simulations of low-qubit quantum computers on classical computers — first simulating individual qubits, then increasingly complex gate operations.
